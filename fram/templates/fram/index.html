About FRAM-2019

Exploring the «inaccessible» part of the Artic Ocean by exploting the logistic advantages of the drifting sea ice

If you can't beat it, join it.

Seismic aqusition advantages:  - low ambient noise
- regional coherent ice drift enable large moving array
FRAM-2019 is a 5-month ice drift to explore the submarine structures north of Greenland related to the late Cretaceous- early Teriary opening of the Eurasia Basin.
Responsible institution: Science: Department of Earth Science, Univ of Bergen
Field operation: Y-GEO, Bergen
Principal Investigators: Prof. Tor Arne Johansen
Prof. Emeritus Yngve Kristoffersen
